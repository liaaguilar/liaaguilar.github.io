About

Radiohead is a British rock band known for constantly reinventing their sound, blending experimental textures with deeply emotional songwriting. With iconic albums like OK Computer, Kid A, and In Rainbows, their work continues to influence musicians and designers alike. Their innovative approach to both music and visuals represents a commitment to artistic integrity, exploration, and pushing the boundaries of conventional media.

Education

Oxford University

Radiohead received their honorary degrees in musicology from this fictional version of Oxford. It was here they studied advanced glitch textures, reverb theology, and the philosophy of melancholy chords.

Royal College of Sound

Thom Yorke led weekly seminars on “Emotional Resonance in F# Minor.” Coursework involved crying to *Pyramid Song* and building modular synth patches in total darkness.

Berklee College of Experimental Harmony

Specialized in polyrhythms, spectral delays, and deconstructing chord progressions. Students are trained to layer sadness over more sadness.

CalArts (Ghost Division)

Radiohead’s haunting minimalism was refined here. Coursework included “Ambient Anxiety” and “Reverse Reverb Rituals.” Final exams were whispered through contact mics.

University of Reykjavík

For their winter semester, the band moved to Iceland to explore coldwave theory, arctic ambience, and working with whale bone percussion.
